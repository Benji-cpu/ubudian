## Learnings
- **Soulfire "Jul 12" was a wrong-year call**: The competitor scout returned "Jul 12, 2026" from an AllEvents listing that explicitly says "July 2025." The day-of-week test caught it: Jul 12, 2026 = Sunday. Monthly Saturday series → correct 2026 dates are Jul 11, Jul 18, or Jul 25. Pattern: ANY date from AllEvents or aggregators for a monthly recurring event should be checked for day-of-week consistency. If it doesn't land on the expected weekday, it's a different year.
- **Biweekly arithmetic + aggregator snippet is sufficient to ingest without a dated slug**: Shambala Jul 25 was ingested without a confirmed Megatix dated slug, relying on biweekly cadence (Jul 11 → +14 = Jul 25), bali.live explicit date confirmation, and day-of-week check (Sat ✓). source_url=null prevents dedup collision with the evergreen slug shared by the Jul 11 entry. Layer-2 dedup (title+start_date) correctly distinguishes both editions. This pattern applies to any biweekly/monthly confirmed series where Megatix blocks direct date extraction.
- **see.events IDs ≠ event IDs — they are organizer IDs**: ID 85110245 is Dragon Tea Temple's organizer ID on see.events, not a single event ID. Multiple events (Kirtan with Diana, Alegria, etc.) share this ID. The slug gets frozen at creation time and survives across event renames — trust the page title over the slug when they diverge.
- **Zest Ubud and Dragon Tea Temple are next-door at Jl. Penestanan No.7 / No.8**: Functionally adjacent, co-organize, cross-promote. Bandsintown venue mapping conflates them. Resolve via the organizer's own ticket page.
- **Bunyi Bumi's anonymous performer approach is deliberate**: The event deliberately avoids names. If this becomes a recurring series with named artists, score would clear the bar. Watch @dragon.tea.temple IG for the "reveal" post typically dropping 3-5 days before.
- **Akasha SuperMoon is a brand name, not tied to the astronomical supermoon**: `supermoon-conscious-party-purnama-x-villa-akasha` references "purnama" (Balinese: full moon) — the event dates to full moons, not specifically supermoons. Don't discard it because the next full moon (Jul 29) isn't an astronomical supermoon. The slug/brand is aspirational naming.
