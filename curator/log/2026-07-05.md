## Learnings
- **Soulfire "Jul 12" was a wrong-year call**: The competitor scout agent returned "Jul 12, 2026" from the AllEvents listing that explicitly says "July 2025." The day-of-week test caught it: Jul 12, 2026 = Sunday. Monthly Saturday series → correct 2026 dates are Jul 11, Jul 18, or Jul 25. Pattern: ANY date that allEvents or aggregators return for a monthly recurring event should be checked for day-of-week consistency before ingesting. If it doesn't land on the expected weekday, it's a different year.
- **see.events IDs ≠ event IDs — they are organizer IDs**: ID 85110245 is Dragon Tea Temple's organizer ID on see.events, not a single event ID. Multiple events (Kirtan with Diana, Alegria, etc.) all share this ID because they're all DTT events. The slug gets frozen at creation time and survives across event renames. When see.events shows a title mismatch between slug and page title, trust the page title — it reflects the current event.
- **Zest Ubud and Dragon Tea Temple are next-door at Jl. Penestanan No.7 / No.8**: Functionally adjacent, co-organize, cross-promote. Bandsintown's venue mapping conflates them. When a Penestanan event appears at "Zest Ubud" on one aggregator and "Dragon Tea Temple" on another, they may be the same physical event at adjacent/shared outdoor space. Resolve via the organizer's own ticket page (Megatix/see.events).
- **Bunyi Bumi's anonymous performer approach is deliberate**: The event description avoids all names. This appears to be a curation choice (creating mystique around the ensemble) rather than incomplete data. If this becomes a recurring series with named artists in future editions, score would clear the bar — watch DTT's Instagram for the "reveal" post that typically drops 3-5 days before the event.
- **Agustian Supriatna's Mantraman DTT series alternates artists per edition**: The recurring monthly/bimonthly "Mantraman" format at Dragon Tea Temple (established Aug 7, 2025) serves as a vehicle for different sacred-music artists each time. Jul 16 = Diana Beauty. Future editions could include Agustian himself or other artists in the DTT orbit (Suara Nusantara, Ecstatic Heaven, etc.).
