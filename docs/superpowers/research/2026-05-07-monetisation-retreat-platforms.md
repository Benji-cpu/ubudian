# Monetisation comps — retreat platform economics

- Date: 2026-05-07
- Scope: real-world commission, pricing, and affiliate-program data we can use to design The Ubudian's retreat-booking monetisation.

## TL;DR

- **Bali retreat pricing is bimodal.** True budget product (Serenity Eco Guesthouse, Firefly) clears at **USD 60–800 / week**. The conscious-community sweet spot we'd actually route — yoga/breathwork/tantra in Ubud — clears at **USD 1,800–3,500 for 5–7 nights**, with luxury (Fivelements, Escape Haven) reaching USD 4,000+.
- **Marketplace commissions cluster at 14–15% baseline, 20–30% for boosted visibility.** Retreat.guru = 14%. BookRetreats / BookYogaRetreats = 15% baseline, with 20/25/30% as paid-visibility tiers. Hosts must absorb (cannot mark up to customer).
- **Affiliate revenue-share is the path of least friction.** BookRetreats' own affiliate program pays **40%/50%/60% of their commission** (tiered by bookings/month) with a **3-month cookie** — that's 6–9% of GMV to us, on their inventory, with zero host onboarding.
- **Realistic numbers for The Ubudian**: at the Bali avg ticket of ~USD 2,200, a 6% effective take = **~USD 132 per booking**. 5/mo = ~USD 660. 20/mo = ~USD 2,640. 100/mo = ~USD 13,200. Direct host deals at 10% would roughly double that but cost weeks of sales work per host.
- **What hosts hate**: opaque algorithm-driven ranking, no markup permitted, slow payouts, generic traffic that doesn't match retreat vibe. Our pitch should lead with *curation + audience-fit*, not lowest commission.

## Detailed findings

### BookRetreats.com

- **Host commission: 15% baseline**, with hosts able to opt up to **20%, 25%, or 30%** to bid for ranking visibility. Higher commission is one of the algorithm's signals; not a direct top-of-results purchase. ([fees](https://help.bookretreats.com/en/articles/2501284-how-do-fees-work-on-bookretreats-com), [ranking](https://help.bookretreats.com/en/articles/11164796-boosting-your-retreat-ranking-with-commission))
- **Plus 3% payment-processing fee**, deducted from payout. Free to list.
- **Hosts may NOT mark up to absorb the commission** — site enforces price parity with host's own site. ([parity policy](https://help.bookretreats.com/en/articles/2510945-can-i-add-your-commission-above-the-price-on-our-website-so-we-can-absorb-the-commission-you-are-charging))
- **Affiliate program** ([source](https://help.bookretreats.com/en/articles/6636500-how-to-be-in-our-affiliate-program)):
  - **40% of their commission** on up to 10 bookings/mo
  - **50%** on 11–30 bookings/mo
  - **60%** on 30+ bookings/mo
  - **3-month cookie**, monthly payouts via PayPal/Wise, free to join, no minimum-payout disclosed.
  - Effective take to us at 15% host commission: **6%** (40% tier) → **9%** (60% tier) of GMV.
- **Sample inventory pricing** ([Bali affordable list](https://bookretreats.com/s/yoga-retreats/affordable-yoga-retreats/bali)): "7 Day Mind-Body Reset" $796, "8 Day Emotional Reset" $886, "7 Day All Inclusive Yoga Spa" $659, ultra-budget "Serenity Eco" 7-day $114. Tantra-segment is materially higher: $1,875–$2,550 for 7 days ([tantra search](https://bookretreats.com/s/yoga-retreats/tantra-yoga/bali)).

### Retreat.guru

- **Host commission: 14% flat** on total retreat price; that 14% is collected as the participant's deposit and the host bills the remaining 86% directly. Free to list, unlimited listings, refunded on cancellation. ([marketplace FAQ](https://go.retreat.guru/marketplace-faq))
- **No public affiliate program found** — Retreat.guru's growth is SEO + B2B software cross-sell, not partner-publisher.
- **Separate RMS software** (booking back-office for centres) is sold per-seat starting **USD 67/mo** for <300 registrations/year ([software pricing](https://software.retreat.guru/pricing)). Not a monetisation lever for us, but worth knowing if Ubud venues complain about admin tooling.
- **Tantra/breathwork inventory in Ubud** is heavily indexed here — `retreat.guru/be/tantra-yoga-retreats/ubud-bali-indonesia` and equivalents — making them a direct comp for the conscious-community niche we want.

### Other retreat aggregators

- **BookYogaRetreats / Tripaneer**: same-house terms as BookRetreats — **15% standard commission + 3% processing**, performance-only. Tripaneer's own partner program exists but the public partner page returned 403 to direct fetch; per [Tripaneer ToS](https://www.bookyogaretreats.com/terms-and-conditions) the model mirrors BookRetreats.
- **Booking.com / Expedia / Airbnb (general OTAs)** charge **15–25%** to lodging hosts and are the ceiling of "what hosts will tolerate". ([OTA fees overview](https://prenohq.com/blog/ota-commission-rates-expedia-booking-com-more/), [2026 OTA guide](https://stayfi.com/vrm-insider/2025/11/04/ota-fees/))
- **Wellbeing Escapes / Health & Fitness Travel** (luxury wellness OTAs) operate on traditional travel-agent commission, typically **10–15%**, but require curated hand-sell.

### Host-side economics (what hosts pay & what they want)

- Effective host take-home on a marketplace booking: **USD ticket × (1 – 0.15 commission – 0.03 processing) ≈ 82%**, before payout/banking fees.
- On a USD 2,200 Ubud retreat, that's **~USD 396 lost per booking** to BookRetreats vs ~USD 308 to Retreat.guru.
- Persistent host complaints (synthesised from Trustpilot reviews + OTA-pattern reporting):
  - **Algorithmic opacity** — boosting commission to 30% with no guaranteed ranking outcome.
  - **Price parity enforcement** prevents recovering the cut from customers.
  - **Generic traffic** — high-yoga-vibe retreats get clicks from price-shopping wellness tourists who don't convert.
  - **Slow / quarterly payouts** in some cases; FX losses on Wise/PayPal.
- What hosts *want* from a curator (per blog/podcast pattern across `bookretreats.com/blog/how-to-run-a-retreat`, retreat-host Reddit threads): **audience fit > volume**. A platform that sends 5 right-fit bookings/month is worth more to a 12-bed Ubud retreat than 50 generic clicks.

### Affiliate program landscape (broader wellness niche)

- Network-distributed wellness affiliates pay **5–15% of net sale**, 30-day cookie typical. Examples: Lululemon (Awin) 5%, Barefoot Yoga (ShareASale) 10%, Wai Lana (ShareASale) up to 15%, Organic Basics (Impact) 10% / 30-day. ([Authority Hacker yoga roundup](https://www.authorityhacker.com/yoga-affiliate-programs/), [Lasso health roundup](https://getlasso.co/niche/health-and-wellness/))
- **Retreat-specific affiliate is rarer than product affiliate**, which is why BookRetreats' 40–60%-of-commission share is unusually generous — they're starved for high-intent referral traffic, which we are uniquely positioned to provide.
- No dedicated Awin/ShareASale/Impact program found for tantra/breathwork niche specifically. Direct host deals are the only way to monetise that segment unless we route via BookRetreats / Retreat.guru's existing tantra inventory.

## Recommendation for The Ubudian

- **Initial commission target: 10% direct from hosts**, OR **6–9% via BookRetreats affiliate** for inventory we don't have a direct relationship with. Frame to hosts as "below the 15% you pay BookRetreats, and routed from a curated Ubud audience."
- **Initial pricing model: free listing + performance commission.** No paid placement at launch — trust is the asset; selling ranking erodes it. Add a paid "featured" slot only once we have 3+ months of conversion data to back the rate.
- **Hybrid stack**: direct deals with the 10–15 marquee Ubud venues (Yoga Barn, Pyramids of Chi, Fivelements, Bambu Indah, Soulshine, etc.), aggregator-affiliate fallback for the long tail. This avoids the "can't list it because we don't have a contract" failure mode.
- **Realistic monthly affiliate revenue at avg Bali ticket ≈ USD 2,200**:

| Bookings / mo | 6% (BookRetreats 40% tier) | 9% (60% tier) | 10% (direct) | 15% (host pays us as primary) |
|--------------:|---------------------------:|--------------:|-------------:|------------------------------:|
| 5             | USD 660                    | USD 990       | USD 1,100    | USD 1,650                     |
| 20            | USD 2,640                  | USD 3,960     | USD 4,400    | USD 6,600                     |
| 100           | USD 13,200                 | USD 19,800    | USD 22,000   | USD 33,000                    |

- Floor case (5/mo @ 6%) covers Vercel + Supabase + a small editorial honorarium. 20/mo @ 10% (USD 4,400) is the threshold where retreat monetisation becomes a meaningful pillar of platform revenue alongside membership.
